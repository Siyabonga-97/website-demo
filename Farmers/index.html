About Us

From Soil to Security

We are a group of motivated TUT IT students committed to promoting sustainable food gardening across South Africa. Our project aims to empower communities to grow their own nutritious food, strengthen food security, and encourage environmental awareness. By combining knowledge, innovation, and teamwork, we strive to create greener spaces and inspire others to take part in building a healthier, more sustainable, and food-secure future for all.

Recommendations & Tips

1. Improve Soil Quality

Start composting food scraps, leaves, and grass to create natural fertilizer. Mix organic matter (compost or manure) into the soil to make it richer. Rotate crops each season to prevent soil exhaustion.

2. Manage Water Wisely

Install rainwater tanks to collect and store water for dry days. Use drip irrigation or watering cans instead of hoses to save water. Water plants early in the morning or late afternoon to reduce evaporation.

3. Control Pests Naturally

Grow marigolds, basil, or mint near vegetables — these help repel pests. Use homemade sprays like a mix of dish soap and water for aphids. Introduce beneficial insects such as ladybugs to eat harmful ones.

4. Strengthen Community Involvement

Involve local schools or youth in planting and harvesting. Create a rotational schedule for watering and weeding. Host community training days on composting or pest control.
